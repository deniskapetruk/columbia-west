Is LA (Los Angeles) Safe?: A Guide for International Students

Los Angeles is an extremely diverse city, which makes it a great place for international students to learn English and truly immerse themselves into American culture. However, Is LA safe? – like any major city, staying safe requires awareness and preparation. Here are some tips to make sure your time in Los Angeles is both enjoyable and secure.

Staying Safe in Los Angeles (LA)

Like any major city around the world there are certain things which you should be aware of and certain areas which you should avoid. That being said- Los Angeles is relatively safe in comparison to other cities in the US and around the world. In a ranking of most dangerous US cities Los Angeles is nowhere to be seen. Thanks to a change in local government, which is dedicated to creating a more safe LA, we have seen a significant decrease in the number of violent crimes. According to the LAPD (Los Angeles Police Department) there has been more than a 25% drop in violent crimes and more than a 50% decrease in gang-related crimes.

1. LA Housing Safety

Finding safe accommodation in Los Angeles is one of the most important steps for international students arriving in Los Angeles. Since it is such a sprawling city, there are neighborhoods to fit every budget and lifestyle, but not all areas offer the same level of safety. When selecting housing, look for clean, well-maintained neighborhoods with good walkability, meaning they are close to shops, cafes, or other conveniences. Additionally, it’s essential to prioritize security features, such as gated entry, surveillance cameras, and well-lit streets at night. For extra peace of mind, avoid sending money or signing a lease without visiting the property in person, as online scams are not uncommon. Once settled, getting to know your neighbors can make your community feel safer and more welcoming. Apps like Nextdoor can help you connect with people in your area and stay updated about local news.

2. LA Transportation Safety

How safe is LA in regards to its public transportation? Los Angeles is famous for its sprawling layout and car culture, but there are still plenty of public transportation options available. The city’s buses and Metro system are affordable, but not always the most safe, particularly late at night or at deserted stations. To ensure your safety, stick to busy routes and avoid traveling alone after dark. For added convenience and security, rideshare apps like Uber or Lyft can be excellent alternatives, but always double-check the driver’s identity and vehicle details before entering the car. If you choose to walk, especially in the evening, stay alert, travel in groups when possible, and keep your personal belongings secure. For example, use a bag with a zipper and avoid storing valuables like your phone or wallet in easy-to-reach places such as your back pocket. If you’re driving, follow all traffic laws and never leave items visible in a parked car, even briefly.

3. Emergency Preparedness

Staying safe in Los Angeles means being aware that you are living in an area prone to earthquakes, so it’s crucial to familiarize yourself with basic earthquake safety protocols. During a quake, remember to “Drop, Cover, and Hold On” by taking cover under a sturdy table or desk and staying away from windows or heavy objects that could fall. It’s also wise to prepare an emergency kit with essentials like water, non-perishable food, a flashlight, batteries, and a first-aid kit. Additionally, stay informed about local hazards such as wildfires, which can occur during the dry season. Many resources, including apps like Citizen, provide real-time safety alerts, ensuring you’re always aware of potential dangers. In any emergency, dialing 911 will connect you with police, fire, or medical services, so memorize this number and have your phone readily accessible.

4. Staying Informed

Ensuring your safety in Los Angeles means staying informed. Local news outlets and weather alerts can help you stay updated on any disruptions, from traffic accidents to weather-related events. When visiting iconic landmarks like Hollywood or Venice Beach, stick to well-populated areas and avoid isolated streets, especially at night. Additionally, if you plan to explore the city’s beaches, swim only in designated areas and follow instructions from lifeguards to avoid strong currents or unsafe conditions. While exploring, keep your phone charged and accessible, as it’s not only useful for navigation but also critical in case of emergencies.

5. Exploring LA and Staying Safe

Los Angeles is home to many iconic tourist spaces, including Hollywood, Santa Monica, and Downtown LA, which attract visitors from all over the world. While these areas are vibrant and exciting, they require vigilance to stay safe. In Hollywood, for example, the Walk of Fame and nearby attractions can be crowded, making them hotspots for pickpocketing. Keep your belongings secure, avoid carrying large amounts of cash, and use a crossbody bag with zippers to deter theft. Santa Monica, with its famous pier and beaches, offers stunning views but comes with its own risks, especially at night. Stick to well-lit areas, avoid isolated parts of the beach, and keep personal items close to prevent theft. In Downtown LA, attractions like the Walt Disney Concert Hall and The Broad museum are surrounded by busy streets. Always use crosswalks, stay alert to traffic, and avoid wandering into less populated areas after dark. Whether you're exploring bustling tourist hotspots or quiet corners, staying aware of your surroundings and planning your routes in advance will ensure a safe and enjoyable experience.

How to stay safe in the US?

1. General Safety Tips

First and foremost, when exploring all cities including Los Angeles, stay aware of your surroundings, especially in new areas. Stick to well-lit, busy streets, and avoid isolated neighborhoods, especially at night. Traveling in groups is always safer, and making sure to keep your phone charged and accessible for emergencies is very important. Avoid listening to music in your earbuds when out and about, because it can reduce your awareness of what is happening around you. Most importantly, don’t forget that your eyes and gut instincts will be your best friends in keeping you safe. If somewhere looks or feels unsafe, it probably is. 

2. Financial and Online Safety

Protect yourself financially by avoiding scams and being cautious with personal information. Do not share sensitive details like your Social Security Number (SSN) or passport information unless it’s completely necessary and only with trusted organizations. If you are unsure of whether or not to share personal information, err on the side of caution. Usually, only scams will ask you to share information such as SSN, bank information, etc over text or the phone.

3. Socializing Safely

When moving somewhere new it’s a given that you will want to meet new people, which can be exciting, but also requires a level of caution. For this reason, always meet in public places until you feel comfortable, and if consuming alcohol, do so responsibly. On this note, when going to bars or clubs never leave your drink unattended, and listen to your instincts in social situations. Also, make sure to only drink drinks that you saw the bartender prepare. 

4. Staying Healthy and Secure

Your physical and mental health are just as important as your personal safety. Make sure to register for health insurance, as medical costs in the U.S. can be expensive without coverage. Additionally, keep a list of nearby healthcare facilities and understand how to access their services in case of illness or injury. Staying healthy also means managing stress effectively—studying abroad can be overwhelming at times, so take breaks, stay connected with loved ones back home, and seek support from school counselors or peers if needed. Maintaining both your health and safety will ensure a successful and enjoyable experience as an international student.

Conclusion

Los Angeles is a city of endless opportunities, and with the right precautions, your experience as an international student can be safe, enriching, and unforgettable. By staying informed and prepared, you’ll enjoy everything this city has to offer while also staying secure.
