THE ISSUE

It’s no secret that what English students really want is to be able to SPEAK English. Every other skill – reading, writing, grammar – is a distant second to the goal of speaking English well.

Unfortunately, countless students waste valuable time, energy and resources in the mistaken belief that studying English abroad in an English-speaking country will automatically improve their speaking skills.

The truth is, learning to speak English boils down to 3 key factors – plenty of Practice, Professional guidance, and the right learning Plan.

Key Factor 1: Practice

Matching Input and Output

Learning to speak English is a skill, and like any other skill, improvement requires lots of speaking PRACTICE – applying the knowledge in your brain (input) by turning it into speech (output). Remember that memorizing grammar points won’t make you a fluent English speaker. Only by practicing those grammar points in real conversations can you truly improve your speaking skills.

Never Enough Speaking Practice

Unfortunately, you'll never get enough speaking practice in a typical English class. Why?

Typical English classes always turn into a lecture, because there's only one teacher and lots of students. If you're sitting in a class listening to your teacher talk most of the time, you might be gaining knowledge (passive learning or input), but how will you ever learn to SPEAK English?

An Entire Class Dedicated to Speaking Practice!

So instead of a boring lecture, ESS students get an entire 80-minute class every day to practice their speaking skills!

What You Need

Real-time Error Correction and Feedback

In addition to targeted, speaking PRACTICE, you also need a trained PROFESSIONAL (an expert teacher) at your side to give you individualized, real-time error correction and feedback as you practice speaking. Otherwise, you'll have no idea what you're doing right or wrong and what you need to improve.

Not Enough Individual Attention

Unfortunately, since one teacher can't give you the individual attention and feedback you need in a typical English class, your speaking skills don't improve.

You end up having to practice speaking with your classmates, but because everyone in the class is at the same English level, no one knows when anyone else is making a mistake and can't give error corrections or feedback.

Trained Teaching Assistants in Your Speaking Class

With trained, native English-speaking Teaching Assistants in your speaking class, you’ll get the real-time error correction and feedback you need to improve your speaking as quickly as possible.
